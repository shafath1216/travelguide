Explore Barisal

Kuakata Beach

Kuakata Beach is famously known as the "Daughter of the Sea" and offers an unparalleled 180-degree view of both sunrise and sunset over the Bay of Bengal. Visitors can enjoy long walks on the sandy shore, local seafood delicacies, and traditional fishing activities. The beach is surrounded by lush greenery, small local villages, and is a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts.

Entry Fee: Free

Floating Market

The Floating Market of Barisal is a vibrant and traditional riverside market where traders sell fruits, vegetables, fish, and handicrafts directly from their boats. Visitors can experience the bustling activity, interact with locals, and even take a boat ride to explore the canals. The market is especially lively during early mornings when fresh produce arrives from surrounding villages.

Entry Fee: Free

Durga Sagar

Durga Sagar is the largest pond in Barisal, spanning over several acres. The site is surrounded by ancient temples and is an important cultural and spiritual center for locals. Visitors can enjoy serene boat rides, witness traditional rituals, and explore the rich biodiversity of the area including migratory birds during certain seasons. It's a peaceful getaway in the heart of the city.

Entry Fee: 20 BDT

Baliatoli Mosque

Baliatoli Mosque is a historic mosque known for its exquisite Mughal-era architecture and intricate carvings. It has served as a spiritual center for the local Muslim community for centuries. The mosque is surrounded by gardens and small markets, offering visitors a blend of cultural heritage, religious history, and traditional Barisal charm.

Entry Fee: Free
