Explore Khulna

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the world’s largest mangrove forest and home to the famous Royal Bengal Tiger. Visitors can explore rivers, canals, and wildlife sanctuaries by boat. It is a paradise for bird watchers, photographers, and nature lovers.

Entry Fee: 150 BDT (Local), 1500 BDT (Foreigners)

Sixty Dome Mosque

The historic Sixty Dome Mosque in Bagerhat is one of the most significant Islamic architectural sites in Bangladesh. Built in the 15th century, it is famous for its massive structure, terracotta design, and serene surroundings.

Entry Fee: 20 BDT

Khan Jahan Ali's Tomb

The tomb of Khan Jahan Ali, the founder of Bagerhat, is a sacred site surrounded by a beautiful lake. It is both a spiritual and historical place where visitors can learn about medieval architecture and local legends.

Entry Fee: Free

Rupsha River

The Rupsha River is an iconic river in Khulna, known for its scenic beauty and boat rides. It plays a major role in the city’s economy and is perfect for sunset views, photography, and enjoying fresh river fish at nearby eateries.

Entry Fee: Free
